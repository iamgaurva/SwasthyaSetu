BMI Chart & Ideal Weight Guide

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a value derived from a person's weight and height. It provides a simple numeric measure of a person's thickness or thinness, allowing health professionals to discuss weight problems more objectively with their patients.

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25–29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater

Age Considerations

While BMI is useful for most adults 20 years old and over, there are some important age factors to consider:

Children and Teens (2-19 years):

BMI is calculated differently for children and teens. Age and gender-specific percentiles are used instead of the adult categories.

Adults (20-65 years):

Standard BMI categories apply. Many health professionals suggest maintaining a BMI between 18.5-24.9 for optimal health.

Older Adults (65+ years):

Some research suggests that for older adults, being slightly overweight (BMI between 25-27) may be protective and associated with better health outcomes.

Weekly Exercise Plans

Bulking Exercise Plan

Focus on heavy compound lifts with progressive overload to maximize muscle growth and strength gains.

Day 1: Chest & Triceps

Barbell Bench Press
  • 4 sets of 6-8 reps
  • Focus on increasing weight progressively
  • Rest 2-3 minutes between sets
Incline Dumbbell Press
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Focus on full range of motion
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
Weighted Dips
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Add weight if possible
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
Skull Crushers
  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Focus on tricep contraction
  • Rest 60 seconds between sets
Cable Tricep Pushdowns
  • 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  • Focus on squeezing at bottom of movement
  • Rest 60 seconds between sets

Day 2: Back & Biceps

Deadlifts
  • 4 sets of 5-6 reps
  • Focus on proper form
  • Rest 3 minutes between sets
Weighted Pull-ups
  • 3 sets of 6-8 reps
  • Add weight if possible
  • Rest 2 minutes between sets
Barbell Rows
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Focus on squeezing shoulder blades
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
Barbell Curls
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Avoid swinging
  • Rest 60 seconds between sets
Hammer Curls
  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Focus on forearm and bicep development
  • Rest 60 seconds between sets

Maintenance Exercise Plan

Balanced workout routine that combines strength training, muscle endurance, and moderate cardio to maintain current physique.

Day 1: Full Body Strength

Squats
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Moderate weight
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
Bench Press
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Moderate weight
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
Bent-Over Rows
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Moderate weight
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
Overhead Press
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Moderate weight
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
20-minute moderate cardio
  • Cycling, jogging, or elliptical
  • Heart rate at 60-70% of max

Day 2: HIIT & Core

HIIT Circuit (20 minutes)
  • 30 seconds work, 30 seconds rest
  • Exercises: Burpees, Mountain Climbers, Jumping Jacks, High Knees
  • 5 rounds total
Planks
  • 3 sets of 45-60 seconds
  • Rest 45 seconds between sets
Russian Twists
  • 3 sets of 20 reps (each side)
  • Use medicine ball or weight if possible
  • Rest 45 seconds between sets
Hanging Leg Raises
  • 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  • Focus on controlled movement
  • Rest 60 seconds between sets

Cutting Exercise Plan

High-intensity workouts that combine resistance training with cardiovascular elements to maximize fat loss while preserving muscle mass.

Day 1: Upper Body & HIIT

Circuit Training (minimal rest between exercises)
  • Push-ups: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
  • Pull-ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  • Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  • Rest 60 seconds between circuits, 3 rounds total
HIIT Cardio
  • 20 seconds sprint, 40 seconds walk/jog
  • 10 rounds (10 minutes total)
Core Finisher
  • Mountain Climbers: 3 sets of 30 seconds
  • Plank: 3 sets of 45 seconds
  • Rest 30 seconds between exercises

Day 2: Lower Body & Cardio

Circuit Training (minimal rest between exercises)
  • Goblet Squats: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
  • Walking Lunges: 3 sets of 12 steps each leg
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  • Calf Raises: 3 sets of 20 reps
  • Rest 60 seconds between circuits, 3 rounds total
Steady State Cardio
  • 30 minutes at moderate intensity
  • Heart rate at 65-75% of max
  • Options: jogging, cycling, stair master, elliptical
Core Finisher
  • Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 20 reps (each side)
  • Russian Twists: 3 sets of 20 reps (each side)
  • Rest 30 seconds between exercises
